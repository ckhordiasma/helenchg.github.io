Welcome to Elena Chong Loo's portfolio Homepage!

I am an undergraduate at the Rose-Hulman Institute of Technology, the #1 ranked undergraduate engineering school that does not have a doctorate program in the United States. I am currently a senior, majoring in electrical engineering with a minor in robotics and mathematics.

Currently, I am a research intern in the "Embodied Interaction" group at the Max-Planck Institute for Informatics, working under the supervision of Dr. Jurgen Steimle.

My goal is to develop innovative interfaces to ease our interaction between our digital information and physical world. My research interests are human-computer interaction, wearable technology, and augmented interfaces. Throughout my undergraduate studies, I have done research and projects on asteroid photometry, photolithography, wireless energy harvesting, 3D printing, sensor and haptic technology, and microwave device coupler fabrication.

I believe that combining science, engineering, mathematics together with art, we can create very innovative approaches. Over the past few years, I have done some creative work that mixes a wide variety of fields together with science and engineering themes, which you can check out on my YouTube channel.
